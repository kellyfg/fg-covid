Reimaging for radical COVID recovery

A local response to COVID-19

Over the past two months, we’ve helped local authorities and NHS partners with their immediate response to the COVID-19 crisis. As we begin to understand the longer term implications of the pandemic, we wanted to share what we’ve been doing, what we’ve learnt and how it might work for you.

What we've done

  • Driving true multidisciplinary working around user needs

    We worked with Camden Council to design a new shielding service and use digital/tech to create a single view of resident needs to foster collaboration in response to COVID-19, providing essentials to the people in need and supporting truly multidisciplinary working across services, the Voluntary Community Sector and the local community to provide support to people at the earliest possible opportunity.

  • Building on the community and asset driven response of COVID-19

    We’re supporting Trafford Council to continue a council wide modernisation programme, building on the community and asset based approaches that have enabled Trafford to meet the needs of residents in response to COVID-19. This work has included using our recent Recovery Maturity Assessment approach to develop recovery recommendations.

  • Developing tools to track and monitor PPE levels

    Working with East London Health and Care Partnership, we developed a digital solution to collecting, monitoring and managing PPE stock levels for both acute and community care providers.

  • Developing new services to triage emerging needs

    Beacon is a multi-agency product that creates a single, unified view of all COVID-19 related requests and needs that have come into councils and the voluntary sector, enabling a more effective and efficient response. It’s fully open source and reusable.

How we can help

Recovery assessment

Understanding lessons and insights from your crisis response. Helping shape priorities, and capitalise on community responses/assets to support future services.

Helping you organise for change
